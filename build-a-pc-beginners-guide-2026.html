7 Costly Mistakes First-Time PC Builders Make

  • Forgetting to flip the power supply switch (the "I/O" switch on the back)
  • Not pushing RAM in hard enough — it takes more force than you'd expect
  • Buying a CPU and motherboard that aren't compatible (use PCPartPicker!)
  • Cheap power supplies — don't go below 80+ Bronze rating, ever
  • Not installing standoff screws before the motherboard — this can short-circuit everything
🎯 Key Takeaway: Building a PC is easier than cooking a complex recipe. Use PCPartPicker for compatibility, follow a YouTube build guide for your specific case, and take your time. A $600 custom build will outperform a $900 pre-built. The satisfaction of pressing power and seeing YOUR creation come to life is priceless.
